D'Farm Dream UI/UX Designer and QA Engineer

A comprehensive redesign and automation project for a legacy system, enhancing usability and accessibility for a better user experience.

Role: QA Engineer & UI/UX Designer
Date: October 2024

Why I Created This Project

Throughout my involvement with D’Farm Dream, Indonesia’s pioneering modern egg-laying poultry farm utilizing an IoT-based system, I faced the challenge of managing a complex agricultural platform that integrated employee workflows, coop and warehouse management, as well as financial and investor monitoring. While the system was powerful and comprehensive, it required improvements in usability and reliability to better support its diverse users.

In my dual role as a QA Engineer and UI/UX Designer, I focused on enhancing the overall user experience by applying responsive and user-friendly design principles using Figma, while rigorously performing manual testing and bug reporting to ensure the system’s stability and quality. My goal was to create an intuitive and efficient platform that allowed investors and farm operators alike to easily track chicken health, feeding, and conditions in real-time, ultimately making daily operations smoother and more error-resistant.

This project provided a valuable opportunity to blend design thinking with quality assurance practices, building a solid foundation for ongoing improvements and future scalability of the application.

Basics Aren't Basic

Often, the simplest elements of a design system—like color, typography, spacing, and component states—are overlooked or poorly implemented. This leads to inconsistencies and difficulties in scaling the design. For D'Farm Dream, I paid meticulous attention to these fundamentals, ensuring they were not only well-defined but also easy to understand and use.

The challenge with many design systems is that they don't get the basics right. This makes them difficult to understand and to build on. One of my main goals was to fix common issues to create a solid foundation to build on. This included establishing clear guidelines for dark mode, responsive grids, and a comprehensive component library that covered all essential UI elements, ensuring predictability and consistency across the application.

A Simple Yet Powerful Colour System

Color systems can often be overly complex and difficult to understand. I wanted my color system to be easy to learn, but flexible enough to scale for complex design systems. I created a concise set of variables and named them based on how colors are used. The name of each colour token consists of up to 4 words that describe how and where to use the colour on an interface.

I decided to use transparent colours, rather than solid ones, to help ensure components look consistent on different coloured backgrounds. This proved crucial in achieving the seamless integration of UI elements across various sections of the D'Farm Dream application, adapting effortlessly to different themes and contexts.

Intuitive Components

One of my biggest gripes with legacy systems is that they can be slow and fiddly to use. To help fix this, I tested prototypes for each component so they're quick and intuitive to customise. I also tested out my design system by creating lots of example interfaces. This helped me iron out the kinks.

There's more than one way to design a component like a dropdown menu or even one as simple as a form input field. Over the years, I've designed and user-tested many variations of different components and this design system is the culmination of that work. Each component has been optimized to make a user-friendly and accessible for users.
